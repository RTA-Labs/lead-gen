# Email Template

Use this template as a guide. Personalize based on the hooks available for each company.

## Subject Line Options
- "Impressed by {{hook}} - quick question about {{company_name}}"
- "{{company_name}} caught my attention"
- "Quick intro - interested in {{company_name}}"

## Body Structure

```
Hi {{first_name}},

[PERSONALIZED OPENER - Use one of these approaches based on available hooks:]
- Reference a specific case study or customer win
- Mention a testimonial or notable customer
- Reference recent news or funding announcement
- Comment on a specific product feature or approach

{{sender_positioning}}

Given what you're building at {{company_name}}, I'd love to learn more about your vision and where you're headed.

Would you have 15-20 minutes for a quick call this week or next?

Best,
{{sender_name}}
{{sender_organization}}
```

## Dynamic Placeholders

- `{{company_name}}` — Target company name
- `{{first_name}}` — Recipient's first name
- `{{hook}}` — Personalization hook (case study, news, customer, etc.)
- `{{sender_positioning}}` — Pulled from thesis.md Outreach Positioning section (Role/Introduction + Value Proposition)
- `{{sender_name}}` — From thesis.md Outreach Positioning
- `{{sender_organization}}` — From thesis.md Outreach Positioning

## Personalization Guidelines

1. **Case Study Hook**: "I was impressed by how you helped [Customer] achieve [Result]. The approach you took to [specific detail] really stood out."

2. **Customer Logo Hook**: "Seeing [Notable Customer] as a customer caught my attention - that's a strong signal of product-market fit in the [industry] space."

3. **News/Funding Hook**: "Congratulations on [recent news/milestone]. It's clear you're building something significant in [space]."

4. **Product Hook**: "Your approach to [specific feature/problem] is refreshing. Most solutions in this space [common approach], but you've taken a different path."

## Tone
- Professional but warm
- Concise (under 150 words ideal)
- Specific, not generic
- Show you've done your homework
- Clear ask (meeting request)

## What NOT to Do
- Don't be generic ("I came across your company...")
- Don't oversell or be too eager
- Don't ask for proprietary information in the first email
- Don't mention competitors negatively
- Don't make assumptions about their business plans
